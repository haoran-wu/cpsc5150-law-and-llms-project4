# Part B Divergence Scenario

Taxpayer: Maya Lopez, age 41.

Other person: Noah Lopez, age 18.

Facts:

- Noah is Maya's son.
- Noah lived with Maya for 12 months of the tax year.
- Noah is a U.S. citizen.
- Maya provided 90% of Noah's support.
- Noah provided only 5% of his own support.
- Noah married late in the year.
- Noah and his spouse filed a joint return.

Why this scenario is useful:

- A quick natural-language answer may focus on Noah being Maya's child, living with her all year, and being mostly supported by her.
- The statute, however, contains a strong joint-return exception in §152(b)(2), which can disqualify Noah as a dependent.

Planned comparison:

- LLM quick answer: likely "dependent" because the obvious family-support facts dominate.
- SMT result: not a dependent because the joint-return exception blocks dependency.
- Manual legal conclusion: the SMT result is legally stronger under the text of §152(b)(2).
